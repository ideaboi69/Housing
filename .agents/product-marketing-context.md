## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Student housing, finally done right.

**What it does:** cribb is a student housing platform that helps university students find verified rentals, sublets, roommates, and move-in essentials in one place. Every listing gets a Cribb Score (0–100) based on price, location, amenities, and real tenant reviews, so students can compare options with confidence instead of renting blind.

**Product category:** Student housing marketplace / off-campus housing platform. The "shelf" students currently shop on includes Facebook groups, Residen, Places4Students, Kijiji, and university classifieds boards (e.g., thecannon.ca at UoG). cribb sits there but with a holistic Cribb Score, real tenant reviews, integrated roommate matching, and a student marketplace.

**Product type:** Two-sided marketplace (students ↔ landlords) with adjacent community + commerce surfaces (roommates, marketplace, blog).

**Business model:**

*Phase 1 — Launch (current):* Free for students. Free baseline for landlords (list properties at no cost). Free roommate matching, free marketplace.

*Phase 2 — Post-traffic monetization (planned):* Paid placement boosts — landlords can pay to move individual listings, sublets, or marketplace items to the top of the relevant feed.

*Phase 3 — Bundled boosts (idea):* For marketplace, users who list 3–5 items together can pay $2–$5 to bundle and boost them together (e.g., a full move-out kit).

Monetization rolls out only after the platform has meaningful traffic. Current focus is volume and trust, not revenue.

**Launch market:** Guelph, ON (University of Guelph). Built in Guelph by Guelph students, currently gated to `@uoguelph.ca` emails.

**Scope:** cribb is a student housing platform — not a Guelph platform. Guelph is the launch beachhead. The vision is a multi-campus Canadian (and beyond) student housing platform.

## Target Audience

**Primary user (demand side):** University students looking for off-campus housing — all years welcome, including post-grad. Within that:

| Cohort | Why they matter |
|--------|-----------------|
| **1st & 2nd years** | Highest intent. New to renting, most likely to feel lost on Facebook/Kijiji, most likely to get burned by a bad landlord. The "I have no idea what I'm doing" audience cribb is built for. |
| **Upper years (3rd, 4th, +)** | Highest intent for **sublets** — co-op terms, summer rooms, study-abroad gaps. Less new-to-renting, more "I need this specific 4-month window covered." |
| **Post-grad / grad students** | Welcomed and encouraged. Same trust + transparency needs; often higher budgets and longer leases. |
| **International students** | Highest stakes — arriving in a new country, often without local network, often doing it sight-unseen. Verification + reviews matter most to this group. |

**Primary user (supply side):** Student-rental landlords.
- Operate 1–10+ properties near campus.
- Currently fragmented across Facebook, Residen, Kijiji, Places4Students, thecannon.ca, and word of mouth.
- Want to fill rooms with less back-and-forth and more pre-qualified leads.

**Secondary users:**
- **Writers** — local creators/businesses contributing content to The Bubble (campus pulse blog).
- **Admins** — internal moderation + verification team.

**Primary use case:** Finding a trustworthy place to live for the next school year (or covering a semester via sublet) without spending weeks bouncing between sketchy sites, ghosted DMs, and lease terms nobody explained.

**Jobs to be done:**
1. **"Help me find a place I won't regret."** Verify the listing is real, the landlord is legit, past tenants didn't hate it.
2. **"Help me decide between options."** Compare listings side by side instead of 14 browser tabs.
3. **"Help me find roommates and fill the place."** Match on lifestyle, form a group, lock in a lease together.
4. **"Help me handle the move."** Buy/sell furniture, find local deals, get oriented to the city.

**Specific scenarios:**
- First-year moving off residence, never signed a lease.
- Group of 4 friends trying to find a 4-bedroom together.
- Co-op student needing a 4-month sublet starting in May.
- International student arriving who needs a place + furniture + everything.
- Upper-year leaving for summer and needs to sublet their room.
- Post-grad doing a one-year program who wants a clean, professional-feeling rental.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Off-campus student housing search is broken. Listings are scattered across Facebook groups and listing boards, landlords ghost, prices and conditions aren't transparent, and students sign leases they don't fully understand because they're under time pressure during peak rental season.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Facebook groups + Marketplace:** The biggest behavioral incumbent. Scam-prone, no verification, hard to compare, conversations live in DMs that disappear, no roommate tooling, no reviews. But it's where everyone already is.
- **Residen:** Closest direct competitor. Already multi-university with claimed 70k+ students. Has student verification, direct booking, and an internal amenity-based listing score for landlords — but no student-facing trust score combining reviews, no real roommate matching, no integrated student marketplace.
- **Places4Students:** Passive listing wall. No quality signal, no scoring, no reviews, no roommate matching, no sublet-specific tooling.
- **Kijiji, thecannon.ca, and similar classifieds:** Bare listing boards. No verification, no scoring, no roommates, no community. Useful only if you already know what you want and trust your gut.
- **University residence:** Safest default, but expensive, restrictive, and unavailable upper years.

**What it costs them:**
- Wasted weeks bouncing between tabs, screenshots, and spreadsheets.
- Signing leases they regret (overpriced, bad landlord, hidden issues).
- Missing peak season and getting the worst remaining options.
- Anxiety, conflict with roommates after move-in, financial loss.

**Emotional tension:** Fear of getting scammed. Anxiety about signing a 12-month commitment with imperfect info. Pressure to lock something down before everyone else does. Feeling stupid for not knowing what they don't know (first-years especially).

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors (same solution, same problem):**

- **Residen (residen.ai)** — The most important direct competitor. Already multi-university across Ontario (Queen's, Waterloo, Western, Mac, Ottawa, Carleton, Brock, Guelph, Trent, Windsor). 70k+ students claimed. Verified student users. Direct booking for viewings. Has an "amenity score" for landlord listings (internal optimization signal, not a student-facing trust score). Strong TikTok presence. **Falls short because:** no real roommate matching, no tenant reviews on listings, no integrated student marketplace, scoring is amenity/landlord-side not a holistic student-side trust signal.

- **Places4Students** — Long-standing campus-affiliated listings board across many Canadian schools. **Falls short because:** passive listing wall, no quality signal, no reviews, no scoring, no roommate matching.

- **Other regional/national portals (Student Housing Canada, RentSeeker.ca, uhomes.com, Canadian Student Living)** — Aggregator-style listing sites. **Fall short because:** no student-side trust score, no roommate tooling, no community layer, mostly geared toward purpose-built or large-operator properties.

**Secondary competitors (different solution, same problem) — the biggest threat by behavior:**

- **Facebook (groups + Marketplace)** — The behavioral default. UofG Off-Campus Housing and similar groups; FB Marketplace for furniture and sublets. Free, high-volume, where everyone already is. **Falls short because:** zero verification, scam-prone, no scoring, no reviews, no roommate matching, conversations vanish into DMs.

- **Kijiji** — Generic rental classifieds. **Falls short because:** not student-aware, no verification, bare-bones.

- **thecannon.ca** (UoG CSA classifieds) — Campus-specific classifieds board for housing + general items. **Falls short because:** plain listing board, no scoring, no verification, no roommate matching.

- **Reddit / Discord / group chats** — Word-of-mouth. **Falls short because:** doesn't scale; only works if you already know upper-years.

**Indirect competitors (conflicting approach):**

- **University residence** — Safest default, but expensive and unavailable upper years.
- **"Just sign whatever"** — Many students end up renting from the first landlord who answers a Facebook DM.

**Important nuance on scoring:** Most platforms have some form of "score" — but they're distance-based or transit-based, or (in Residen's case) amenity-based for landlord optimization. **No competitor offers a holistic, student-facing Cribb Score that combines price + location + amenities + real tenant reviews into a single 0–100 trust signal.**

**Verification:** Cribb has identity-verified landlords (document upload + Textract extraction) and email-gated students. Most competitors verify one side or neither. Trust gap is real and exploitable.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**

1. **Cribb Score — the only student-facing holistic trust score.** Other platforms score distance, transit, or amenities (for landlord-side optimization). Cribb combines price, location, amenities, and real tenant reviews into one 0–100 number that's color-coded for fast scanning. No one else does this.

2. **Real roommate matching, not a side feature.** This is genuinely uncategorical — no other student housing platform has a proper compatibility quiz, group formation, invite/request flow, and the ability to link a formed group to a listing. Residen and Places4Students don't. Facebook doesn't.

3. **Two-sided verification.** Landlord identity verified via document upload + Textract extraction. Students gated by university email. Most competitors verify one side or neither.

4. **Bundled ecosystem, not a bolt-on.** Listings + sublets + roommate matching + student marketplace + campus blog (The Bubble) in one app. Competitors are listing-walls with at most one adjacent feature.

5. **Built by students who lived the problem.** Founders are CS students at UoG. Same school they're solving for. Brand reflects this — student-to-student, no corporate filter.

6. **Honest brand voice.** Explicitly rejects fake social proof ("Trusted by 2,400+ students" was removed in development). Trust comes from real reviews and verified accounts, not manufactured numbers.

**How we do it differently:** Treat housing as a multi-step decision (find → compare → form group → sign → move in → live there) and build for each step, not just the listing-display step.

**Why that's better:** Students don't have to glue together 5 different tools (Facebook + Residen + Discord + Kijiji + spreadsheets). They get scoring to cut through noise, group tools to coordinate roommates, and trust signals so they can move faster with less regret.

**Why customers choose us over alternatives:** Once you see a Cribb Score and read real reviews on a listing, going back to a bare-bones listing wall or a Facebook group feels like flying blind.

## Objections

**Lead objection (most common, expected): "What actually differentiates cribb from Residen / Facebook / Places4Students? What do you bring to the table?"**

This is the #1 question cribb needs to be able to answer in one breath. The answer:

> Three things no one else does together. **One:** a student-facing Cribb Score that combines price, location, amenities, and real tenant reviews — not just distance or transit. **Two:** actual roommate matching with a compatibility quiz, groups, and invites — nobody else has this. **Three:** verified landlords *and* verified students on the same platform, with reviews tying it all together.

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| **"What makes cribb different from Residen, Facebook, Places4Students, etc.?"** | Cribb Score (real tenant reviews + price + location + amenities, not just distance) + actual roommate matching with groups + two-sided verification. No other platform bundles all three. |
| "There aren't enough listings yet" (early-market) | Lead with quality + verification. Every cribb listing is verified, scored, and reviewable. 50 trusted listings beat 5,000 sketchy ones. Inventory grows with each landlord onboarded. |
| "I already use Facebook groups, it's free" | Cribb is also free for students. The difference: Facebook doesn't tell you if a landlord ghosts, overcharges, or has 1-star reviews. Cribb does. |
| "I don't trust the Cribb Score — it's just a number" | The score is transparent. You can see every input: price, location, amenities, reviews. New listings are scored on property data alone until reviews come in. Score updates as reviews accumulate. |
| ⚠️ Additional objections heard in conversations | (to be filled in as you hear them — e.g., from landlords on pricing, from students on inventory, etc.) |

**Anti-persona (who is NOT a good fit):**

## Notes for Other Skills

When other marketing skills (cold-email, community-marketing, launch-strategy, etc.) reference this document:

- **Default to "student housing platform" framing**, not "Guelph student housing." Guelph is the launch market and a major proof point ("built by Guelph students, currently live at UoG") — not the scope.
- **The Cribb Score is the headline product feature.** Lead with it, and clarify it's *holistic* (price + location + amenities + reviews) — competitors only score on distance/transit/amenities.
- **Roommate matching is the second-strongest differentiator.** Nobody else has real roommate tooling — emphasize this against Residen specifically.
- **Two-sided platform** — when writing copy, be clear whether the audience is students or landlords; messaging is very different.
- **Residen is the primary competitive frame for direct comparisons.** Facebook is the primary competitive frame for behavior change ("stop using FB groups").
- **Honest > polished.** The brand has explicitly rejected fake social proof. Don't generate "trusted by N+" placeholders or stock-photo testimonials.
- **Monetization is post-launch.** Free for students and landlords today. Don't generate copy mentioning paid boosts as if they're live; they're roadmap.
- **Founder story is real and on-brand.** David and OJ, CS students at UoG, "built the platform we wish existed in first year." Use this when humanizing.
